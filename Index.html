Life Insurance Study Guide

Basic Terms

Life Insurance: Money paid to family when someone dies.
Death Benefit: Money the beneficiary receives when the insured dies.
Premium: The payment for an insurance policy.
Insured: Person whose life is covered.
Beneficiary: Person who receives the death benefit.
Policy Owner: Person who controls the policy.
Face Amount: Basic coverage amount of the policy.
Policy Term: How long the policy lasts.
Contestability Period: Time the insurer can challenge the policy (usually 2 years).
Exclusions: Situations where the policy does not pay.
Suicide Clause: Policy won’t pay if insured commits suicide within a certain time.

Types of Life Insurance

Term Life Insurance: Coverage for a set number of years.
Whole Life Insurance: Coverage that lasts the insured’s entire life.
Universal Life Insurance: Flexible premiums and adjustable death benefit.
Variable Life Insurance: Cash value invested in stocks/bonds; death benefit may vary.
Variable Universal Life (VUL): Combines variable investment and universal flexibility.
Endowment Policy: Pays a lump sum if insured survives to a certain age.
Joint Life Insurance: Covers two people; pays on first death.
Survivorship Life Insurance: Pays death benefit after both insureds die.
Single Premium Life: Paid once; coverage lasts for life.

Policy Features & Benefits

Cash Value: Savings inside permanent insurance.
Policy Loan: Borrowing money from the cash value.
Surrender Value: Cash received if the policy is canceled.
Rider: Optional addition to a policy.
Waiver of Premium: Waives premiums if insured becomes disabled.
Accelerated Death Benefit: Pays part of death benefit early if terminally ill.
Nonforfeiture Options: Choices to retain some benefits if premiums stop.
Level Premium: Premium stays the same over the life of the policy.
Flexible Premium: Premiums can be increased or decreased.
Convertible Term: Term insurance that can be converted to permanent insurance.
Renewable Term: Term insurance that can be renewed without medical exam.
Premium Mode: How often premiums are paid (monthly, quarterly, annually).
Dividends: Extra money returned to policyholders of participating policies.
Settlement Options: Different ways death benefit can be paid.
Contingent Beneficiary: Receives benefit if primary beneficiary is unavailable.
Third-Party Ownership: Policy owned by someone other than the insured.

Insurance Practices & Rules

Underwriting: Assessing risk to determine insurability.
Insurable Interest: Financial interest in the life of the insured.
Preferred Risk: Low-risk, healthy individual.
Standard Risk: Average risk.
Substandard Risk: Higher risk due to health or lifestyle.
Twisting: Misleading someone to replace a policy (illegal).
Rebating: Giving incentives to sell insurance (illegal).
Assignment: Transferring policy rights to someone else.
Beneficiary Designation: Naming person(s) to receive the death benefit.
Policyholder: Person who owns the policy.
Impaired Risk: Higher risk due to health conditions.
Moral Hazard: Risk created by insured’s behavior.
Physical Hazard: Risk from lifestyle or physical condition.
Adverse Selection: High-risk people buying insurance more often.

Policy Timing & Payment

Grace Period: Extra time to pay late premium.
Free Look Period: Time to cancel policy and get a refund.
Premium Deposit Fund: Money held by insurer to pay future premiums.
Cost of Insurance (COI): The charge for providing coverage.
Interest-Sensitive Life: Premiums and cash value affected by interest rates.
Family Income Policy: Combines term insurance with income benefits.
Family Maintenance Policy: Provides income for a set period after death.
Joint and Last Survivor: Pays after both insureds die.
Viator: Person selling life insurance for cash (viatical settlement).
Mortality Table: Statistical table to calculate life insurance rates.
Policy Illustration: Document showing projected policy performance.
Lapse: Policy ends due to non-payment.
Reinstatement: Restoring a lapsed policy.
Loans vs Withdrawals: Borrowing cash value vs taking money out permanently.
Excess Interest: Additional interest earned above guaranteed amount.
Medical Exam: Test insurers use to assess health risk.

Additional Riders & Advanced Terms

Annuitant: Person receiving payments from an annuity.
Accidental Death Benefit Rider: Extra payout if death is accidental.
Disability Income Rider: Pays if insured becomes disabled.
Guaranteed Insurability Rider: Buy more coverage later without exam.
Cost of Living Rider: Increases death benefit with inflation.
Long-Term Care Rider: Pays for long-term care if needed.
Policy Loan Interest: Interest charged on loans against cash value.
Partial Withdrawal: Taking money out of a permanent policy.
Cash Surrender Value: Cash you receive when canceling a permanent policy.
Death Benefit Options A/B: Level or increasing death benefit structure.
Automatic Premium Loan: Uses cash value to pay missed premiums automatically.
Return of Premium Rider: Refunds premiums paid if insured survives term.
Modified Endowment Contract (MEC): Tax classification for overfunded policies.
Split Dollar Life Insurance: Arrangement where premiums are shared by employer and employee.
Collateral Assignment: Using policy as collateral for a loan.
Indexed Universal Life: Cash value grows based on stock index performance.
Guaranteed Minimum Death Benefit: Death benefit won’t fall below guaranteed amount.
